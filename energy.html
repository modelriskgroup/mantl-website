Save Your Energy

Cut your lift power.
Keep your barrels.

Electricity is one of the largest controllable costs on a producing well — and it's also an emissions line on your ESG report. MANTL's reported field studies show a 54.2% average reduction in electricity consumption with no production losses. Here's why, and what it could mean for your wells.

Why PCPs use less power

Efficiency is built into the physics.

No reciprocating mass

Unlike beam pumps, a PCP turns continuously — no counterbalance losses, no lifting and dropping heavy steel on every stroke.

Pump-off control

Our in-house automation matches production to inflow, eliminating the over-pumping that quietly burns electricity.

Right-sized systems

A pump engineered to the well, not oversized "to be safe," draws only the power the job actually needs.

ESG & reporting

Lower power is lower emissions.

Every kilowatt-hour you don't burn is an operating-cost saving and a Scope 2 emissions reduction you can report. For producers under decarbonization pressure, efficient artificial lift is one of the few moves that improves opex and ESG at the same time.

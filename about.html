Our story

Born in the downturn.
Built to give a s#!t.

In 2014, as oil fell from over $100 to the low $40s, ten oilfield professionals did the opposite of what the market was doing — they started a company. They'd spent years watching consolidation pull service further from the wellsite, and they were done watching.

The independent the field needed.

MANTL's founders had all relied on progressing cavity pumps as their preferred lift method. As bigger players bought up the competition, the service that producers actually depended on got worse. So MANTL was built locally-owned, independent, and customer-centric — and started with four field stores across Alberta and Saskatchewan in the teeth of the worst market in a generation.

A decade later, that bet looks like a company: 100+ people, 10 field stores, three Alberta manufacturing shops, in-house design and automation, and progressing cavity pumps running in roughly 15 countries. Led by president and CEO Matthew Kenna, MANTL still runs on the principle it was founded on — show up, solve it, and give a s#!t.
